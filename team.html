Lab Manager 

Qimin Liu, Ph.D.

Qimin Liu is an assistant professor at Boston University Department of Psychological and Brain Sciences. He completed health service psychology internship at University of Illinois Chicago Department of Psychiatry. He earned Ph.D. in Psychology with specializations in Quantitative Methods and Clinical Science from Vanderbilt University. He also holds a M.S. degree in Applied and Computational Mathematics and Statistics from University of Notre Dame. His Bachelor degrees in psychology, mathematics, and philosophy were from University of Washington.

Lauren Trichtinger, Ph.D.

Lauren Trichtinger is an Assistant Professor of Statistics and Data Science at Simmons University. Lauren earned her Ph.D. in Quantitative Psychology from the University of Notre Dame. Her research interests include developing statistical methods for analyzing big data, particularly intensive longitudinal data.

Merna Alghannam

Hi! I am Merna Alghannam, a computer science graduate from Boston University. With a solid foundation in Computer Science and an interest for Data Science, my internship at MIT in Computational Social Science was a natural progression, leading to an interest in quantitative psychology after further personal research on the area. I thrive on dissecting large datasets, seeking patterns in the noise—skills that are integral to both fields. Quantitative psychology fascinates me as it employs rigorous data analysis and statistical methods to decipher the numerical underpinnings of human thoughts and behaviors. It represents a compelling challenge: to apply and develop statistical tools to the soft unpredictability of psychological phenomena, enabling a data-driven understanding of the human experience.

1/17/24 update: All pre-interviews for 2024 Fall PhD admission have been conducted. Please note that I only reviewed applications that listed me first as the faculty of interest. Per program policies, I am not able to respond to inquiries regarding the outcome or progress of your application. Updates related to the decisions will be sent via official program/university channels. 

Due to the volume of applications, I won't be able to offer individual feedback. You are welcome to check back in April for my general feedback/reflection for all applicants. Many factors can contribute to the success of your application, many of which are not reflective of your competitiveness as an applicant. There are quite a few applicants that I hoped to interview but will not be able to: As the formal interviews require resources beyond my own from the entire program, there is a limitation on how many candidates I can invite to the interview. There is a small chance that I will be able to invite more candidates to the interview at a later date but there is no guarantee. 

Many of you have the potential to become, if not already are, a great scientist. I have no doubt that many of you will be accepted into PhD programs.  In the unlikely event that you are not, I sincerely hope that you will consider applying to my lab again in the future.  Regardless, please keep us in mind for collaborations and future post-doc opportunities! Thank you for expressing an interest in my lab.

As Volunteer Research Assistant

We are looking for research assistants to join us for the Spring 2024 semester. If you're interested in being a research assistant for our lab, please fill out the Google Form. We particularly welcome individuals with data science or programming experiences, and/or with research interests in sexual gender minority health, and/or from disadvantaged backgrounds. 

As PhD Student

Dr. Liu is reviewing applications this cycle for a Ph.D. student to start in Fall 2024. Application details are listed on the department website. Interested applicants are encouraged to view the frequently asked questions prepared by Dr. Liu and are welcome to comment anonymously there or email for additional questions. 

As Post-Doctoral Fellow

We are open for a postdoc fellow to join for a minimum of 2 years. Interested individuals with experiences in Monte Carlo simulation, statistical software development, and natural language processing/affective computing are particularly encouraged to reach out.  Opportunities for additional supervised clinical experiences are possible through CARD.

As Research Staff

We currently do not have any openings for paid research staff positions. You are welcome to check back at a later time. BU students, however, are welcome to apply for stipends via UROP.

For Directed Study [BU Students Only]

We are open to BU undergraduate and masters students to complete directed study with Dr. Liu. Undergraduate or Masters students who are interested in doing a directed study should reach out with unofficial undergraduate transcripts and curriculum vitae/resume attached.  More information on directed study is available for undergraduate and for masters students.

For Honors Thesis [BU Students Only]

We are open to BU undergraduate student to complete honors thesis with Dr. Liu. The Honors in Psychology program is an opportunity for senior psychology majors to undertake a yearlong research project. Students who wish to pursue Honors in Psychology must submit an application May 31st prior to the senior year's first semester. If you are interested, please reach out with unofficial transcript and curriculum vitae/resume attached.
